# AB Flow Candidates - GPT

## Scope

`AB_flow_*` tools may compose accepted `A_flow_*`, accepted `B_flow_*`, and narrowly scoped direct `A_atom_*` / `B_atom_*` calls when a full accepted flow would be too broad. AB flows are the verified workflow layer: observe or discover with A, perform safety/state-control/calibration/reset/path actions with B, then use A again to verify, drain errors, capture status, or log teardown context.

`AB_flow_*` tools must not call future `C_atom_*` tools and must not run IV/CV/pulse/WGFMU measurement recipes. They may read pre-existing direct B1500A output buffers or pre-existing EasyEXPERT latest results through accepted A flows, but they must not create measurement data by starting a measurement. Any flow that selects and executes a measurement recipe, defines sweep/source waveforms, or interprets measurement results as device characteristics is out of scope.

AB flows should make safety and observability explicit. They can report that a B safety action was executed and then A status/error evidence was collected, but they must still distinguish `verified_zero_outputs`, `error_queue_drained`, `session_closed`, `calibration_ran`, and `measurement_ready` as separate claims.

## Rejected / Too Broad AB_flow Candidates

- `AB_flow_full_station_startup_all_transports`: too broad because direct B1500A, WGFMU, and EasyEXPERT have separate transports and failure modes. Compose smaller P0 flows at the application layer.
- `AB_flow_run_idvg_after_preflight`: requires future C measurement atoms for recipe selection/execution and result interpretation.
- `AB_flow_run_cv_after_cmu_correction`: CMU correction can be AB, but CV execution/result generation is C scope.
- `AB_flow_program_wgfmu_waveform_and_measure`: waveform creation/execution and data generation are future C scope.
- `AB_flow_easyexpert_select_run_fetch_application_test`: selecting/running EasyEXPERT tests needs new B/C classification and execution atoms; fetching results alone is A.
- `AB_flow_auto_recover_any_failure`: too broad and unsafe; recovery should be explicit emergency, reset, safe-disconnect, or endpoint-specific recovery flows.
- `AB_flow_enable_channels_for_measurement_readiness`: channel enable may be B, but robust readiness needs compliance/range validation and C recipe context that do not exist yet.
- `AB_flow_calibrate_everything`: too broad across B1500A, WGFMU, CMU, QSCV, and EasyEXPERT zero-cancel; use endpoint-specific maintenance/calibration flows.
- `AB_flow_disconnect_all_sessions`: too broad and mixes direct B1500A, WGFMU, and EasyEXPERT teardown semantics.
- `AB_flow_collect_and_classify_measurement_results`: reading pre-existing buffers/results is A, but classification as device measurement output belongs to future C/analysis layers.
- `AB_flow_easyexpert_zero_cancel_then_fetch_existing_result`: defer as too context-sensitive for now; the fetched latest result may be stale or unrelated to the zero-cancel action.

## Missing Atoms / Flows Noted During Planning

- `A_atom_query_service_request_mask`: needed to make `AB_flow_configure_status_polling_with_baseline` reversible.
- `A_atom_serial_poll_status_byte`: useful for AB emergency/status flows that need interrupt-style clearing semantics rather than `*STB?`.
- `A_atom_wgfmu_get_status` and `A_atom_wgfmu_get_channel_status`: needed for stronger WGFMU AB verification after initialize, self-test, calibration, or close preparation.
- `A_atom_easyexpert_get_selected_test_name`: useful to audit EasyEXPERT abort flows without running or selecting tests.
- `B_atom_confirm_channel_disabled` and `B_atom_query_channel_output_state`: needed to strengthen safe-disconnect and zero/disable verification beyond zero-voltage confirmation.
- `B_atom_set_channel_compliance_limits`: needed before any future AB channel-arm flow can be considered safe.
- `B_atom_validate_channels_for_state_change`: needed for ASU/SCUU/channel operations when station-profile validation should be B-native.
- `B_atom_query_asu_path` and `B_atom_query_scuu_path`: needed to directly verify path switching and support restore flows.
- `B_atom_query_cmu_correction_state` and `B_atom_query_cmu_phase_compensation_mode`: needed for reversible CMU calibration brackets.
- `B_atom_wgfmu_abort_operation`: needed for a true WGFMU emergency AB recovery flow.
- `B_atom_easyexpert_query_standby_state` and `B_atom_easyexpert_query_selected_measurement_state`: needed for idempotent EasyEXPERT abort/standby verification.
- `B_flow_run_b1500_diagnostics_suite`: deferred in B arbitration until a diagnostic item catalog exists; would be useful inside future AB diagnostics flows.

## Design Notes

- Naming: use endpoint and outcome names, for example `AB_flow_safe_disconnect_b1500`, `AB_flow_wgfmu_open_baseline_self_check`, and `AB_flow_easyexpert_abort_standby_recover`. Do not encode numeric ordering in names.
- Return schema: include `flow`, `fake`, `hardware_touched`, `ok`, `partial`, `phase_results`, `validation_checkpoints`, `failure_handling_taken`, `warnings`, `cautions`, `operator_acknowledgements`, `flows_called`, `atoms_called`, and raw per-flow/per-atom results.
- Phase model: represent each AB flow as `observe`, `act`, and `verify` phases. Emergency flows may start with `act` to avoid delaying safety action.
- Error semantics: distinguish communication failure, safety-action failure, verification failure, calibration failure, and stale/no-result conditions. A failed A verification should not erase successful B safety evidence.
- Idempotency: discovery/status/error-read phases are generally repeatable, but error drains consume queues, buffer preparation can clear data, resets destroy settings, calibration/correction changes state, and path switching affects fixtures. Inputs should make those effects explicit.
- Audit logging: preserve pre-action and post-action snapshots when practical. For destructive actions, store operator acknowledgements, reason strings, timestamp/session metadata, and all raw atom/flow results.
- Failure policy: default to stopping after failed validation before state-changing B actions. For emergency flows, default to attempting zero/disable even if abort fails. Never escalate automatically from zero/disable to reset/calibration unless the flow name and inputs explicitly request it.
- Verification limits: AB can log stronger evidence than A or B alone, but it still cannot certify DUT safety, station wiring, or measurement validity without external station profile and future C recipe validation.
- Parallelism: execute instrument or remote-session commands serially unless the driver later provides an audited batching/concurrency layer. Client-side formatting of returned logs may run in parallel after command phases finish.
